Things I notice that make me go "oh, that's clever." Sometimes it's branding. Sometimes it's a button. Sometimes it's a portable charger.

Logo design
Boulder Movement — a fist that's also two letters

Look at their logo and you'll see a clenched fist, perfect for a climbing gym. Look again and you'll find a lowercase 'b' and 'm' hidden in the negative space. It's doing three things at once: sport, letters, brand.

Logo design
The Meating Place — a cow that's also M and P

A protein restaurant with a logo that's the silhouette of a cow. Look closely and the outline forms the letters 'm' and 'p'. The wordplay in the name is fun on its own, but the logo makes it land harder.

Usability
The lift close button that confused everyone

Stood in a lift for way too long because nobody could tell which button was "close door." Took really long for the doors to close because we couldn't figure it out. This is just amusing, but also: icon design matters.

Industrial design
The portable charger that thought of everything (almost)

USB plugs on one end, inbuilt short wires on the other, and the handle doubles as a charging cable. Truly life-saving portable charger that accompanied me through Europe. One flaw: the wires can't loop back to charge the charger itself. So close.

Logo design
The joy of finding letters inside letters

Some logos just show you the name. The good ones make the name do something extra: a hidden letter, a shape within a shape, a double meaning you spot on the second look. Once you start noticing it, you can't stop.
