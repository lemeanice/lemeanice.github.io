The project
Helping young people find volunteering that fits their lives

On My Way! is the National Youth Council's platform for connecting youth with volunteering opportunities across Singapore. The challenge was that young people were aware volunteering existed, but the discovery and commitment process felt like too much friction.

My role during the internship was focused on the early research and framing phase: building out user personas based on interviews and secondary research, and mapping the journey from "I want to volunteer" through to actually showing up.

Due to NDA constraints, I'm not able to share specific research outputs or internal work products here. But the project is live. The platform is at discover.nyc.gov.sg/omw/Partner-With-Us.

What I worked on
Persona and journey mapping
Personas
Youth volunteer archetypes
Built out user personas representing different types of young volunteers, varying by motivation, schedule constraints, and prior volunteering experience. These grounded later design decisions in real user context.
Journey mapping
Discovery to commitment arc
Mapped the full journey from awareness through sign-up and first attendance. Identified where drop-off was most likely and what touchpoints most influenced whether someone followed through.
